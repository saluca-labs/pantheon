## TL;DR

You may say what Pantheon is. You may not present your product as Pantheon.

## What you may do without permission

- Refer to Pantheon factually in articles, talks, blog posts, comparison
  tables, and documentation ("Pantheon is a governance-first AI security
  platform", "compatible with Pantheon", "based on Pantheon").
- Describe yourself as a contributor to, or maintainer of, Pantheon when that
  is accurate.
- Use the unmodified Pantheon name in nominative references where there is
  no reasonable substitute, provided you do not imply endorsement, sponsorship,
  affiliation, or certification by the Pantheon project.
- Reproduce screenshots and short excerpts of Pantheon documentation under
  fair use, with attribution.

## What requires our written permission

- Using "Pantheon" (alone or in combination) as part of a product name,
  service name, company name, or project name. Examples that require
  permission and will generally be refused: "MyCo Pantheon", "PantheonPro",
  "Pantheon Cloud", "Pantheon Hosted", "Open Pantheon".
- Distributing the Pantheon logo, wordmark, or other Pantheon visual assets,
  including modified versions, recolored versions, or derivative marks.
- Registering domain names, social media handles, package names, or
  trademarks that contain "Pantheon" as a distinctive element.
- Selling merchandise (apparel, stickers, etc.) bearing the Pantheon name
  or marks.
- Any use that implies official endorsement, certification, or
  affiliation with the Pantheon project.

## Forks and downstream distributions

The FSL-1.1-Apache license permits you to fork and modify Pantheon's source
code. The Pantheon trademark policy is independent of that grant: a fork is
free to exist, but it must be renamed.

- Acceptable: "MyFork - based on Pantheon", with a clear factual reference
  to the upstream project in your README.
- Not acceptable: shipping a fork under the "Pantheon" name, the Pantheon
  logo, or any confusingly similar name or mark.

This requirement protects users from confusion about what is and is not the
official Pantheon project. It is a standard pattern used by Linux, Mozilla,
Apache, and most other major open-source projects.

You may not redistribute, modify, or use these assets in your own materials
